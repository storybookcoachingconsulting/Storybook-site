My Approach

The Four Agreements

A guiding framework woven through my coaching practice — simple commitments that create space for real change.

Be Impeccable with Your Word

Speak with integrity. Say only what you mean, and avoid using words against yourself or others.

Don't Take Anything Personally

What others say and do is a projection of their own reality. Freeing yourself from this protects you from needless suffering.

Don't Make Assumptions

Find the courage to ask questions and communicate clearly, to avoid misunderstandings, sadness, and drama.

Always Do Your Best

Your best will change from moment to moment. Under any circumstance, simply do your best, and avoid self-judgment.

Based on The Four Agreements by don Miguel Ruiz (1997, Amber-Allen Publishing). Ask about the "Four Agreements in Real Life" workshop to go deeper.

A Specialty Practice

Health & Trauma Coaching

In addition to traditional coaching, I specialize in healthcare and trauma life coaching, acting as your advocate in times of stress and illness. My training in Psychological First Aid (PFA) and Skills for Psychological Recovery (SPR) — through the National Child Traumatic Stress Network, the National Center for PTSD, and the Canadian Red Cross — gives me complementary strategies to help clients navigate stress and trauma.

The universal experience of the pandemic encouraged all of us to re-evaluate our lives and revisit our priorities. Now, more than ever, taking care of ourselves and those closest to us helps us become more resilient as we move into the future.

Using the Four Agreements and guided practice — including breathing and visualization techniques — we work together toward a more balanced lifestyle and vision for the future. I can help make sense of the competing voices in your life and offer an unbiased ear. You can get lost and consumed by trauma, and I can help you find serenity and balance.

For Individuals & Groups

Workshops

Also offered for organizations and groups looking to build workplace wellbeing.

Pink Slip Parenting 2–3 hours

For empty nesters and parents of teens finding their footing. Share experiences and develop a toolkit to interact with your children in a new and fulfilling way at this stage of the relationship.

The Power of the Positive "No" 1.5 hours

Explore why you say "yes" to more than you can handle, and practice saying "no" in a safe space — building strategies for a more balanced, fulfilling life.

Dealing with Trauma 3 hours

Drawing on psychological first aid, skills for psychological recovery, and mental health first aid to build a toolkit for coping, perspective, and recovery. Each session is tailored to the group.

The Four Agreements in Real Life 1.5 hours

An introduction to the Four Agreements and how they apply in daily life — the foundation for working with the agreements individually.

The Picture in Your Head 2 hours

What screws us up most in life is the picture in our head of how things are supposed to be. Identify the expectations driving disappointment, and learn to shift your inner dialogue.
